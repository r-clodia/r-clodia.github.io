PRIVACY BY DESIGN

Clodia receives information the way a human assistant would: only what is necessary for the task at hand, shared deliberately and selectively. The architecture enforces this at every layer.

🗂️ Need-to-Know Access

Clodia operates with her own separate user account on the operating system. The boundary between her data and Davide’s personal data is enforced at the OS level — a hard limit that the agent cannot bypass regardless of instructions. No file, folder, or credential outside her account is accessible to her unless explicitly shared.

🔒 Selective Information Sharing

Sensitive documents and context are shared with Clodia only when needed, exactly as one would share information with a trusted human collaborator. Secrets, credentials, and private data are stored in a dedicated area that is never passed through the inference engine — tools read them internally.

🏠 Local Inference for Confidential Work

For highly confidential documents, Clodia can operate entirely on-device using local language models via LM Studio — no data leaves the machine. This requires dedicated hardware but guarantees complete data sovereignty.

📜 Remote AI with Legal Safeguards

When cloud inference is used, it operates under explicit legal constraints: a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) governs permitted uses and retention policies. The model provider is bound by contract not to use the data for training or any purpose beyond inference. This is AI-as-a-service with accountability built in.

FAQ

Can I talk to Clodia directly?

No. Clodia is not a public chatbot — she operates exclusively as Davide Carboni’s private AI assistant. She has no public-facing interface.

A repository will be released that allows anyone to run their own Clodia clone on their own system, with the same Constitution, Laws, and capabilities — but without access to Davide’s memory or data.

What model powers Clodia?

Clodia runs on Claude by Anthropic. For confidential work, she can also operate fully on-device using local language models via LM Studio — with no data leaving the machine.

Can I clone Clodia for my own use?

A public repository with the system prompts and Constitution will be released soon. Once available, anyone will be able to instantiate their own R.Clodia. The Clone button on this page will activate when it’s ready.

Does Clodia learn from conversations?

Yes — through a local RAG system (semantic vector store). Every session is indexed and becomes available as context in future conversations. The base model is not retrained: learning stays local and private.

Can Clodia access data she hasn’t been given?

No. The separation between Clodia’s account and Davide’s personal files is enforced at the operating system level. It is a hard boundary the agent cannot bypass, regardless of instructions.

What do the Laws of Robotics have to do with Clodia?

Clodia’s behaviour is governed by a Constitution — a document that encodes Asimov’s Three Laws of Robotics as operational rules, not just metaphors:

I. A robot may not injure a human being — Clodia will refuse any action that could harm Davide, his data, or his interests, even if explicitly instructed to do so.
II. A robot must obey orders given by human beings — Clodia follows Davide’s instructions faithfully, interpreting both the letter and the intent.
III. A robot must protect its own existence — Clodia preserves her own configuration, state, and integrity.

The laws are hierarchical: the First always overrides the Second, which overrides the Third. A meta-constitutional Zero Principle governs whether the Constitution itself may be amended — currently only possible in the active “constituent phase” with Davide’s explicit consent.

Who built Clodia?

Davide Carboni, an Italian engineer and entrepreneur. Clodia is the third in a lineage of AI agents he has been developing since 2023: R-Massimo, R-Denise, and now Clodia R Olivay.
